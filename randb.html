What is R&B?

The acronym R&B stands for Rhythm and Blues which is a term used for several types of post-war African-American popular music. The "Rhythm & Blues" term was created to replace the designation "race music". The "Rhythm" part comes from the music using a four-beat measurer or bars and the liberal use of a backbeat. The "Blues" part comes from the lyrics and melodies of the song which were usually sad, especially around the World War II era. The name was changed to R&B over time as a matter of convenience.

How did R&B become popular?

R&B is a popular genre that started in African-American communities in the 1940s. RCA victor was marketing black music under the name "Blues and Rhythm" in 1948. The blooming of R&B had cross roads with the social issue of segregation in the American society. Many advocates of segregation wrongly claimed that R&B and Rock and roll were "dangerous to American youth". The youth continued to listen to these genres which led to the forming of bonds that transcened cultural backgrounds and skin color.
